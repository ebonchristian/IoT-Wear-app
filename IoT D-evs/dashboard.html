Pulse Rate

The pulse is usually taken from the radial artery, which can be felt at the wrist, where a watchstrap usually sits, in line with the base of the thumb. It should be felt with two or three fingers of the other hand.

Pulse Oximetry

Pulse oximetry is a test used to measure the oxygen level (oxygen saturation) of the blood. It is an easy, painless measure of how well oxygen is being sent to parts of your body furthest from your heart, such as the arms and legs.

Note

Remember, individual health needs may vary, so it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice and guidance on managing your heart rate and oxygen levels effectively. They can provide recommendations specific to your health status and help you make informed decisions about your well-being.
